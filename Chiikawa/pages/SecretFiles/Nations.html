Nations of the World

The Chiikawa Reich

The Chiikawa Reich stands as a symbol of ambition and unity. Its cities are filled with grand architecture and lively markets. The Reich is governed by a council led by Chiika, whose vision is to bring prosperity to all. The people are proud, valuing discipline and tradition, and their flag flies high over every town square.

Hachiware Republic

The Hachiware Republic is a beacon of creativity and freedom. Its government is democratic, with leaders chosen for their wisdom and compassion. The Republic’s cities are adorned with murals and sculptures, and its people are known for their open-mindedness and love of debate. Festivals celebrate the arts, and every citizen is encouraged to express themselves.

Usagi Union

Spanning the snowy north, the Usagi Union is a federation of resilient communities. Governed by a council of elders, the Union values cooperation and mutual aid. Its towns are built to withstand the harshest winters, and the people are renowned for their endurance and ingenuity. The Union’s flag, a symbol of hope, is raised at every gathering.

Kurimanju Kingdom

The Kurimanju Kingdom is ruled by a wise monarch, whose lineage stretches back centuries. The kingdom is famous for its fertile lands and innovative spirit. Castles and libraries dot the landscape, and the people are celebrated for their hospitality and intellect. The monarchy works closely with local councils to ensure peace and prosperity.

Momonga Empire

High above the clouds, the Momonga Empire is a land of dreamers and inventors. The empire is led by an emperor who encourages exploration and discovery. Its cities are marvels of engineering, with windmills and sky bridges connecting neighborhoods. The Momonga are known for their adventurous spirit and their welcoming attitude toward outsiders.

Rakko

Rakko is a maritime nation, ruled by a council of captains and explorers. Its harbors are filled with ships from every corner of the world, and its people are skilled sailors and traders. Rakko’s government values freedom and enterprise, and its flag, adorned with waves and stars, represents the nation’s connection to the sea.

Diplomacy and Rivalry

The nations of Chiikawa are bound by treaties, alliances, and sometimes rivalries. Diplomatic summits are held in grand halls, where leaders debate, negotiate, and celebrate their shared history. Though differences exist, the nations strive for peace, knowing that unity is the key to a brighter future.
